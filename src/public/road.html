History

The enthusiasm for Road cycling competitions began at the end of the XIX century and has not stopped growing since. Some big races attract around tens of million spectators and are watched by TV viewers all over the world. The first race officially recorded dates back to 31 May 1868 at the ‘Parc de Saint-Cloud’, in Paris, and was won by the British rider, James Moore. These early races include...

  • Liège–Bastogne–Liège (established 1892)
  • Paris–Roubaix (1896)
  • Tour de France (1903)
  • Milan–San Remo and Giro di Lombardia (1905)
  • Giro d'Italia (1909)
  • Tour of Flanders (1913)

    Tour de France

    The Tour de France is one of the most famous cycling tours in the history of bike racing. It first started in 1903 and has continued till now (2025) Each stage is a day-long event, spanning a total of 23 to 24 days. The race covers about 3,500 kilometres (2,200 miles) and features 20 to 22 teams, each with eight cyclists, competing on diverse terrains throughout the competition. The most recentwinner was Tadej Pogacar who aslo won the 2024 World Road Championships - Giro De Italia and the 2023 Vueleta a Espana.

Bike Racing


Cycling is a great sport to do as a proffesion for fitness and health or just for fun !! Some forms of cylcing racing include...

Road Cycling - Riding on paved roads at high speeds competitively with other riders. Mountain Biking - Off road cycling on rough terrain includong gravel, hills, mud/dirt paths and grass Track cycling - Racing on a velodrome a oval shaped track in a competitive environment. Cyclocross - A mix of road cycling and mountian biking, typically ridden on a cross country circuit with obstacles and a varied terrain.

Road

Road Racing
- Competitive races ridden on a road bike and held on paved roads. Road races test a riders endurance, strategy and technical skills. Different types of road races include...
  • One-Day Races (Classics)
  • Stage Races (ranging between 3-21 days of racing)
  • Time Trails (on your own for a certain distance or time)
  • Crit racing (short fast races held in city centers and on a short course)

    Track

    Track Racing
    - Competitive racing that takes place on a velodrome, a specially designed oval track with steeply banked sides. It focuses on speed, power, and strategy and you ride on a fixed gear - meaning you don't change gears.
  • Sprints (extremely fast for a short amount of time)
  • Match Sprints (Two riders racing head-to-head)
  • Omnium - Points Race - Scratch Race - Madison - Elimination
  • Team (Team Sprints and Team Pursuit)

    Cyclocross

    Cross Racing
    Cyclocross is a unique form of off-road bicycle racing that blends elements of road cycling, mountain biking, and running into one. It takes place on a closed circuit (made using tape, stakes and netting) with mixed surfaces, (including dirt, mud, grass, sand, gravel, and sometimes snow or ice) The courses feature obstacles like barriers, stairs, and steep inclines, requiring riders to dismount and carry their bikes. Cyclocross is a winter sport, with the season generally running from September to February
